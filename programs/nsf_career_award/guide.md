# NSF CAREER Award Sponsor Guide

## 1. Executive Summary

The Faculty Early Career Development (CAREER) Program is the National Science Foundation's (NSF) most prestigious award in support of early-career faculty. It aims to identify and support individuals who have the potential to serve as academic role models in research and education and to lead advances in the mission of their department or organization. Activities pursued by early-career faculty should build a firm foundation for a lifetime of leadership in integrating education and research. NSF especially encourages women, members of underrepresented minority groups, and persons with disabilities to apply.

The CAREER program also includes the description of the NSF component of the Presidential Early Career Awards for Scientists and Engineers (PECASE).

## 2. Program Overview

The CAREER Program is a Foundation-wide activity that supports early-career faculty in integrating their research and education activities. The program seeks to foster innovative developments in science and technology, increase awareness of careers in science and engineering, give recognition to the scientific missions of participating agencies, enhance connections between fundamental research and national goals, and highlight the importance of science and technology for the Nation’s future.

NSF funds research and education in most fields of science and engineering through grants and cooperative agreements to approximately 3,000 institutions. NSF accounts for about one-fourth of Federal support to Institutions of Higher Education (IHEs) for basic research. NSF encourages broadening participation in STEM, including leadership, partnership, and contributions from individuals and institutions from communities that are underrepresented and/or underserved in STEM.

**Presidential Early Career Awards for Scientists and Engineers (PECASE):**
Each year, NSF selects nominees for the PECASE from among the most meritorious recent CAREER awardees. Individuals cannot apply for PECASE; these awards are initiated by participating federal agencies. At NSF, up to twenty-six nominees are selected annually from PECASE-eligible CAREER awardees most likely to become leaders in academic research and education. The White House Office of Science and Technology Policy makes the final selection and announcement.

Selection for PECASE is based on two criteria:
1.  Performance of innovative research at the frontiers of science, engineering, and technology that is relevant to the mission of the sponsoring organization or agency.
2.  Community service demonstrated through scientific leadership, education, or community outreach.

To be eligible for a PECASE Award, an individual must be a U.S. citizen, national, or permanent resident. PECASE awards at NSF are honorary and carry no additional funding.

## 4. Eligibility

**Principal Investigator (PI) Eligibility:**
*   Must hold the title of assistant professor or equivalent. Non-tenured associate professors are not eligible.
*   Must hold a doctoral degree by the proposal submission deadline. Defending a thesis without receiving the official, dated diploma by the deadline does not qualify.
*   Untenured assistant professors who have received notification for tenure and/or promotion effective *after* the proposal submission deadline are eligible, provided they meet all other criteria on the deadline date.
*   Previous employment in a tenured position does not affect eligibility if the PI is not currently in a tenured position.
*   CAREER eligibility is not limited by time from degree or years in a tenure-track appointment.
*   U.S. Permanent Resident, U.S. Citizen, or U.S. National status is not required to apply for a CAREER award.
*   An eligible Principal Investigator may submit only one CAREER proposal per annual competition.
*   No co-Principal Investigators (co-PIs) are permitted on a CAREER proposal.
*   You may submit a CAREER proposal if you have NSF or other Federal awards, but the proposed work must not overlap with previously funded projects.
*   You may not submit a CAREER proposal if you have already received a CAREER award.
*   You may participate in at most three CAREER competitions.

**Institutional Eligibility:**
*   Must be employed at an eligible U.S. organization.
*   Non-degree-granting organizations (e.g., museums, observatories, research labs) are eligible if they are non-profit, located in the United States, and the PI holds at least a 50% tenure-track or tenure-track-equivalent appointment with substantial research and educational responsibilities.
*   2-year colleges or community colleges are eligible, provided the PI's appointment meets the CAREER eligibility requirements.
*   It is not possible to apply for the CAREER Program under the Research in Undergraduate Institutions (RUI) provisions; proposers must choose between submitting to RUI or CAREER.

**Eligibility Certification:**
*   Eligibility for the CAREER award is certified by the Departmental Letter. The Department Head is responsible for verifying that all eligibility requirements are met.
*   Through submission of the CAREER proposal, the institution also attests to the proposer's eligibility.

## 5. Award Size & Budget

*   **Duration:** CAREER awards are typically for a 5-year period.
*   **Minimum Award Size:**
    *   The minimum CAREER award size is expected to be **$400,000**, including indirect costs, for a 5-year period.
    *   For proposals submitted to the Directorate for Biological Sciences (BIO), the Directorate for Engineering (ENG), and the Office of Polar Programs (OPP), the minimum award size is expected to be **$500,000** (approximately $100,000 per year) for the 5-year duration.
*   **Maximum Award Size:** There is no stated maximum award size, but proposers are encouraged to be realistic about budget expectations and what has been funded previously in their program. The average CAREER award size varies across NSF.
*   **Budget Preparation:**
    *   The budget request should reflect the scope of the research and education plans and the practices within the PI's discipline.
    *   **Academic Year Salary:** PIs with heavy teaching responsibilities or those whose proposed activities involve a limited period of fieldwork or other extraordinary career-development activity in research or education may be justified in requesting academic year salary support. PIs should contact their disciplinary Program Officer to discuss individual cases before including academic year salary support.
    *   **Education Component Funds:** Adequate resources to carry out proposed educational activities may be requested. These costs should be listed in the proposal budget, and the budget justification should indicate how the funds will be used. This may include materials, participant stipends and travel, and for K-12 education, teacher stipends or substitute pay.
*   **Estimated Number of Awards:** Approximately 500 awards per year.

## 6. How Proposals are Reviewed

NSF evaluates proposals through a competitive merit review process. Most proposers are notified of a decision within six months of submission. PIs receive unattributed verbatim copies of peer reviews.

**PECASE Selection Criteria:**
For PECASE consideration, selection is based on:
1.  Performance of innovative research at the frontiers of science, engineering, and technology that is relevant to the mission of the sponsoring organization or agency.
2.  Community service demonstrated through scientific leadership, education, or community outreach.

## 7. Application Requirements

All proposals must be submitted in accordance with the requirements specified in the CAREER funding opportunity and the NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide (PAPPG) that is in effect for the relevant due date. It is the responsibility of the proposer to ensure the proposal meets these requirements.

*   **Submission Platforms:** Proposals must be submitted via Research.gov or Grants.gov.
*   **Personnel:**
    *   A Principal Investigator (PI) is required.
    *   No co-Principal Investigators (co-PIs) are permitted.
*   **Page Limits:**
    *   The Project Description, including "Results from Prior NSF Support" (if applicable), may not exceed 15 pages.
    *   Visual materials (charts, graphs, maps, photographs, pictorial presentations) are included in the 15-page limitation.
    *   URLs must not be used in the Project Description as they could circumvent page limitations, reviewers are not obligated to view them, and sites could be altered or deleted. The Project Description must be self-contained.
*   **Required Forms/Documents:**
    *   **Departmental Letter:** This letter is mandatory and certifies the PI's eligibility. It must be included in the Supplementary Documents section. Proposals missing this letter will be returned without review.
    *   **Results from Prior NSF Support:** If the PI has served as a PI or co-PI on any NSF grant active in the past five years (including postdoctoral fellowships, conferences, EAGER or RAPID grants, and equipment grants like MRI), a "Results from Prior Support" section must be included. If there are multiple prior awards, report on the one most closely related to the CAREER proposal.
    *   **Letters of Collaboration:** Specific details of any proposed collaborations should be described in the Project Description or in the Facilities, Equipment, and Other Resources sections. Letters of collaboration, limited to a single-sentence format stating intent to collaborate and/or commit resources as detailed in the proposal, may be submitted. These should be scanned and placed in the Supplementary Documents section after the departmental letter. No other documentation (e.g., biographical sketches, appendices) for collaborators is permitted. Letters of recommendation or other statements of support for the project are not allowed.
    *   **PECASE Eligibility Statement (Optional):** To be considered for the PECASE honorary award, upload a Single Copy Document stating: "I wish to be considered for the PECASE honorary award." This confirms meeting PECASE eligibility requirements (U.S. citizen, national, or permanent resident). If eligibility is met after an award is made, this statement can be submitted via an Interim Project Report (IPR) in Research.gov.
*   **Education Component:** The proposal must include a well-argued and specific plan for integrated research and educational activities over a 5-year period. These plans should reflect the PI's disciplinary and educational interests and goals, as well as the interests and needs of their organization. The research and educational activities do not need to be addressed separately if their relationship is better served by interspersing them throughout the Project Description. Excellence in both research and education is expected, but not an unreasonable workload (e.g., teaching additional courses is not expected).
*   **Research Security Policies:** Updates on NSF research security policies include a research security training requirement, Malign Foreign Talent Recruitment Program annual certification requirement, prohibition on Confucius institutes, and an updated FFDR reporting and submission timeline. Proposers must be aware of the definition of a "Malign Foreign Talent Recruitment Program" as detailed in the PAPPG.

**Common Reasons for Proposals Being Returned Without Review:**
*   A co-principal investigator is listed on the cover page.
*   Departmental Letter is missing.
*   Proposal submission occurs after the deadline.
*   Proposal topic is not appropriate for NSF.
*   Project description is missing "Results from Prior NSF Support" section, if applicable.

## 8. Tips for Successful Proposals

*   **Contact a Program Officer:** Your primary contact is a Program Officer in a division or program closest to your area of research. Consult the NSF website's Program Areas, staff directory, or the CAREER Contacts page. Searching the NSF Award Search tool for funded projects in related areas can help identify relevant programs and Program Officers.
*   **Assess Readiness:** Discuss your readiness and career development with your Department Chair, mentor, or an NSF Program Officer before applying. The most important consideration is being ready to write a proposal with an innovative research idea and an education plan aligned with your department's mission.
*   **Early Submission:** NSF strongly encourages early submission of proposals to allow time for file updates or corrections before the deadline. Slow response from the IT Help Desk is expected as the deadline approaches and cannot be used as an excuse for late submission.
*   **Thorough Review:** Thoroughly review your proposal before submission, including identifying any PDF printing problems. Download a copy of the submitted version to ensure it contains all parts and is compliant. If a problem is noticed before the deadline, use Research.gov (or Fastlane for Grants.gov submitters) proposal file update capabilities.
*   **Multidisciplinary Proposals:** If your proposal fits in two or more programs, designate more than one program on the cover sheet. Select the most relevant program as the primary organization. Contact the Program Officer(s) most closely related to the subject matter when preparing a cross-disciplinary proposal. Do not submit duplicate CAREER proposals to multiple disciplinary programs.
*   **Integrated Research and Education:** Clearly articulate how your research and educational activities are integrated and how they will build a firm foundation for a lifetime of contributions.
