# NSF Designing Materials to Revolutionize and Engineer our Future (DMREF) Sponsor Guide

## 1. Executive Summary

The Designing Materials to Revolutionize and Engineer our Future (DMREF) program is the principal NSF program responsive to the National Science and Technology Council’s (NSTC’s) Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP) Subcommittee on the Materials Genome Initiative (MGI). DMREF seeks to foster the design, discovery, and development of materials to accelerate their path to deployment by harnessing the power of data and computational tools in concert with experiment and theory.

The program emphasizes a deep integration of experiments, computation, and theory; the use of accessible digital data across the materials development continuum; and strengthening connections among theorists, computational scientists, data scientists, mathematicians, statisticians, and experimentalists, as well as those from academia, industry, and government. DMREF is committed to the education and training of a next-generation materials research and development (R&D) workforce.

This solicitation (NSF 25-508) is open to all materials research topics and supports activities that significantly accelerate the materials discovery-to-use timeline by building the fundamental knowledge base needed to advance the design, development, or manufacturability of materials with desirable properties or functionality. Awards are expected to range from $1,500,000 – $2,000,000 over a duration of four years. The DMREF program anticipates continuing with competitions biennially in odd-numbered years.

## 2. Program Overview

DMREF supports materials design and development through the integration of experiments, computation, and data-driven methods, while fostering interdisciplinary collaboration and training the next generation of the materials R&D workforce. It aligns with the 2021 MGI Strategic Plan and its three primary goals:
*   Unifying the Materials Innovation Infrastructure (MII), a framework of integrating advanced modeling, computational and experimental tools, and quantitative data.
*   Harnessing the power of materials data.
*   Educating, training, and connecting a world-class materials R&D workforce.

The program supports activities that significantly accelerate the materials discovery-to-use timeline by building the fundamental knowledge base needed to advance the design, development, or manufacturability of materials with desirable properties or functionality. This involves promoting integration and iteration of knowledge across the entire path to deployment through:
*   Building a vibrant research community.
*   Forming interdisciplinary teams to conduct research in a “closed-loop” fashion.
*   Leveraging data science and machine learning.
*   Providing ready access to materials data.
*   Educating the future MGI workforce.

**Key Research Requirements:**
*   Proposals must be directed by a team of at least two Senior/Key Personnel with complementary expertise.
*   The proposed research must involve a collaborative and iterative “closed-loop” process wherein theory guides computational simulation, computational simulation guides experiments, and experimental observation further guides theory.
*   Integrated research activities could involve strategies to advance fundamental knowledge through testing methodology (novel synthetic approaches, innovative processing, advanced characterization), theory/computation/modeling leveraging ML/AI/data mining, automated/high-throughput/autonomous experimentation, and validation through synthesis, growth, processing, characterization, and/or device demonstration.

**Crosscutting Activity and Partnerships:**
This solicitation is a crosscutting activity involving the Directorates for Mathematical and Physical Sciences (MPS), Engineering (ENG), Computer & Information Science & Engineering (CISE), and Technology, Innovation and Partnerships (TIP).

DMREF also involves partnerships with federal and international agencies:
*   **Federal Partners:** Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL), Department of Energy’s (DOE) Office of Energy Efficiency & Renewable Energy (EERE), Office of Naval Research (ONR), National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), US Army Combat Capabilities Development Command (DEVCOM) Ground Vehicle Systems Center (GVSC), and DEVCOM Army Research Laboratory (ARL).
*   **International Partners:** United States-Israel Binational Science Foundation (BSF), India’s Department of Science and Technology (DST), Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada (NSERC), and Germany’s Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft (DFG).

DMREF encourages input and participation from the full spectrum of diverse talent, including underrepresented and underserved communities, aligning with NSF's mandate to broaden participation in STEM.

## 4. Eligibility

**Who May Submit Proposals:**
*   **Institutions of Higher Education (IHEs):** Two- and four-year IHEs (including community colleges) accredited in, and having a campus located in the US, acting on behalf of their faculty members.
*   **International Branch Campuses of US IHEs:** If funding is for an international branch campus, the proposer must explain the benefit(s) to the project and justify why activities cannot be performed at the US campus.

**Who May Serve as PI:**
By the submission deadline, any PI, co-PI, or other Senior/Key project personnel must hold either:
*   A tenured or tenure-track position, or
*   A primary, full-time, paid appointment in a research or teaching position with exceptions granted for family or medical leave, as determined by the submitting institution.

**Limitations on PI/Co-PIs with Prior DMREF Awards:**
*   An investigator who is a PI or co-PI of a DMREF award based on a proposal submitted in response to the previous DMREF Solicitation (NSF 23-530) cannot be a PI or co-PI for this DMREF solicitation, but may serve as Senior/Key Personnel. Proposals violating this limitation will be returned without review.

**Limit on Number of Proposals per Organization:**
*   Each organization is limited to serving as **lead** on five (5) DMREF proposals. If an organization exceeds this limit, the first five proposals based on submission time will be accepted, and the rest returned without review.
*   There is no limit on the number of proposals in which an organization may serve in a Collaborative, non-lead role.

**Limit on Number of Proposals per PI or Co-PI:**
*   No individual may appear as Senior/Key Personnel (PI, Co-PI, Faculty or Other Senior/Key Personnel) on more than one (1) DMREF proposal submitted in response to this solicitation. Any proposal submitted with an individual exceeding this limit after the first will be returned without review. Changes in investigator roles post-submission to meet eligibility requirements will not be allowed.
*   Individuals from DMREF's international (or federal) partners should not be listed on the cover sheet of the NSF proposal and are not considered NSF Senior/Key Personnel.

**Specific International Partner Eligibility:**
*   **Israel (DMREF-BSF):** An individual from Israel may participate in only one DMREF-BSF proposal.
*   **India (DMREF-DST):** Investigators from India may participate in only one DMREF-DST proposal as a DST PI. Co-PIs from India can be involved in up to two DMREF-DST proposals. DST investigators involved in the implementation of two or more projects previously supported by the DST International Group and not expected to be completed by December 31, 2025, are not eligible to be a member of a research team.
*   **Canada (DMREF-NSERC):** Investigators from Canada may participate in only one DMREF-NSERC proposal as the Applicant (Canadian principal investigator) but may be co-applicants (co-PI) on more than one project.
*   **Germany (DMREF-DFG):** There is no limit to the number of DMREF-DFG projects to which a German investigator can participate. Proposals involving private companies whose participation requires a cooperation agreement under DFG rules are not eligible.

**Additional Eligibility Criteria:**
*   All DMREF proposals must involve at least two Senior/Key Personnel (PI, Co-PI, Faculty or Other Senior/Key Personnel) with complementary expertise to cover all aspects of the project (synthesis/growth/processing, characterization/testing, theory/data/computation/simulation).
*   Proposals must not duplicate or be substantially similar to other proposals funded or concurrently under consideration by NSF, or previously declined by NSF and not substantially revised.

## 5. Award Size & Budget

**Anticipated Funding Amount:** $40,000,000 (pending availability of funds). Funds will be partitioned among participating Divisions and are not pooled. Co-funding by multiple Divisions is anticipated for the majority of projects.

**Anticipated Type of Award:** Standard Grant or Continuing Grant.

**Estimated Number of Awards:** 20 to 25, depending on fund availability and proposal quality.

**Award Duration:** Four years.

**Award Size (NSF Component):**
*   Awards are expected to range from **$1,500,000 – $2,000,000** over a duration of four years.
*   The budget must be commensurate with the scope of the project and thoroughly justified.

**International Component Award Sizes (Additional Funding):**
*   **US-Israel Collaborative Projects (BSF):** Maximum award size for the Israeli portion is **$380,000** for a single Israeli investigator or **$568,000** for two or more Israeli investigators (over four years).
*   **US-India Collaborative Projects (DST):** Maximum award size for the Indian portion is up to **20 million Indian Rupees**.
*   **US-Canada Collaborative Projects (NSERC):** Maximum award size for the Canadian portion is **$100,000 (CAD) per year**, totaling a maximum of **$400,000 (CAD)** over four years.
*   **US-Germany Collaborative Projects (DFG):** Expected funding from DFG for the German component should be approximately **100,000 Euro per year** (including direct project costs). The DFG funding period is limited to three years, requiring a no-cost extension or continuation proposal for the fourth year.

**Budgetary Requirements and Limitations:**
*   **Cost Sharing:** Inclusion of voluntary committed cost sharing is **prohibited**.
*   **Indirect Cost (F&A) Limitations:** Not Applicable.
*   **Travel:** Proposed budgets should include funds for travel by one PI or co-PI to attend a biennial MGI Principal Investigator Meeting held in the Washington, DC area in even-numbered years.
*   **Federal Partner Travel:** Proposals partnering with AFRL or EERE may budget funds for travel to required biennial technical review meetings at AFRL headquarters in Dayton, OH, or a venue designated by EERE.
*   **Federal/International Partner Funding:** No NSF funds may be budgeted or sub-awarded to federal or international partners; these personnel participate as non-NSF funded collaborators. The budget may include funds, such as student support or travel, to enable these activities.
*   **Data Management:** The Budget may include funds to support implementation of the data management and sharing plan.
*   **Cloud Computing Resources:** Proposals may request cloud computing resources through CloudBank. The total budget of the project, including any cloud computing resource request from CloudBank, may not exceed the solicitation's budget limit ($2,000,000). The cost of cloud computing resources requested from CloudBank should **not** be included in the NSF budget pages but specified in a Supplementary Document. For example, if a PI requests $20,000 in cloud resources, the NSF budget should be no more than $1,980,000.

## 6. How Proposals are Reviewed

Proposals submitted to DMREF will undergo a merit review process incorporating both technical aspects and broader societal contributions. Reviewers will be asked to consider what the proposers want to do, why they want to do it, how they plan to do it, how they will know if they succeed, and what benefits could accrue if the project is successful.

**Merit Review Criteria (National Science Board approved):**
1.  **Intellectual Merit:** The potential to advance knowledge and understanding within its own field or across different fields.
2.  **Broader Impacts:** The potential to benefit society and contribute to the achievement of specific, desired societal outcomes. This includes, but is not limited to: full participation of underrepresented groups in STEM; improved STEM education; increased public scientific literacy; improved well-being; development of a diverse STEM workforce; increased partnerships; improved national security; increased economic competitiveness; and enhanced research/education infrastructure.

**Elements considered for both criteria:**
*   Potential to advance knowledge/understanding and benefit society.
*   Creativity, originality, or potentially transformative concepts.
*   Well-reasoned, well-organized plan with a mechanism to assess success.
*   Qualifications of the individual, team, or organization.
*   Adequacy of resources available to the PI.

**Additional Solicitation Specific Review Criteria (for all DMREF proposals):**
1.  **Acceleration of Materials Discovery/Development:** How effectively does the proposed work help accelerate materials discovery, understanding, and/or development by building the fundamental knowledge base needed to progress toward designing and making materials with specific, desired functions or properties?
2.  **Iterative Feedback Loop:** How effectively does the proposed research use collaborative processes with iterative feedback among tasks? Do the materials synthesis/growth/processing techniques, characterization/testing methodology, theory/mathematics, data science, and computation/simulation aspects of the project strongly interact with each other to promote significant advances in each of these components and advance materials design?
3.  **Workforce Training:** How effectively does the proposed work provide training for the next generation of scientists and engineers, educated in a multidisciplinary, integrated experimental and computational approach to materials research? Will adequate data-related training be provided for students and postdoctoral researchers, as needed?
4.  **Data Management and Sharing Plan:** How appropriate is the Data Management and Sharing Plan for the type of data expected to be created? How effectively does the proposal convey that the digital data generated by the project will be made freely available within a reasonable time from publication, without the need for request to the investigator, in a way that the data is findable, accessible, interoperable, and reusable (FAIR)?

**Specific Review for DMS Proposals:**
*   Proposals submitted to the Division of Mathematical Sciences (DMS) as the Primary Unit of Consideration will also be evaluated on whether they seek new mathematical or statistical results that will advance the DMREF agenda.

**Review Process:**
*   Proposals will be reviewed by Ad hoc Review and/or Panel Review.
*   NSF will manage the review process. Representatives from federal and international partner agencies may view proposals, recommend reviewers, and attend panels as observers.
*   PIs will receive verbatim copies of reviews (excluding reviewer identities) and an explanation of the funding decision. NSF strives to provide decisions within six months.

## 7. Application Requirements

All proposals must be submitted in accordance with the requirements specified in this funding opportunity (NSF 25-508) and the NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide (PAPPG) that is in effect for the relevant due date. Proposers may submit via Research.gov or Grants.gov.

**General Proposal Preparation Instructions:**

*   **Proposal Set-up:**
    *   Select "Prepare New Full Proposal" in Research.gov.
    *   Search for and select solicitation NSF 25-508.
    *   In Step 2, "Where to Apply," select at least one participating NSF Directorate and Division as the primary unit. Additional Directorates/Divisions may be selected.
    *   In Step 3, "Proposal Type," select "Research" or "GOALI" as appropriate.
    *   For collaborative proposals submitted as separate submissions from multiple organizations via Research.gov, all proposals in the collaborative group must be directed to the same participating Directorate/Division(s) in the same sequence. Do NOT select "separately submitted collaborative proposal" unless more than one U.S.-based institution is submitting for separate funding.
    *   Collaborative GOALI proposals must be submitted as a single proposal with collaborators identified as subawardee organizations.

*   **Proposal Title:**
    *   Must begin with '**DMREF:**' followed by the project title.
    *   Collaborative proposals from different institutions must begin with '**Collaborative Research: DMREF:**'.
    *   GOALI proposals must begin with '**GOALI: DMREF:**'.
    *   Proposals with an international partner must begin with '**DMREF: NSF-international funding agency:**' (e.g., 'DMREF: NSF-BSF:', 'Collaborative Research: DMREF: NSF-BSF:', or 'GOALI: DMREF: NSF-BSF:').

*   **Personnel:** Personnel associated with, or to be supported by, federal or international agencies partnering with DMREF must **not** be listed on the cover sheet or designated as Senior/Key Personnel on the NSF proposal.

*   **Project Summary:**
    *   The final line of the Overview portion must be a list of no more than five key words preceded by the phrase or heading **Key Words**.
    *   If requesting cloud resources through CloudBank, include "**CloudAccess**" as one of these Key Words.
    *   If partnering with federal agencies, use the following as Key Words: **AFRL, EERE, ONR, NIST, GVSC, or ARL**.

*   **Project Description (15-page limit):**
    *   Clearly present the proposed work and its connection to DMREF objectives.
    *   Articulate the data and software cyberinfrastructure, as well as research infrastructure, planned for use.
    *   Specify plans to train and develop a diverse and inclusive MGI workforce.
    *   For collaborations with federal and/or international partners, explain how the collaboration benefits both the DMREF team and the partner in advancing project goals and MGI.
    *   Must include a brief description of the management plan preceded by the phrase or heading **Management Plan**. The plan should describe integration of project components (data, workforce development) into an effective iterative feedback loop, especially for multi-institution projects. Define roles of all Senior/Key Personnel and any federal/international personnel. Include a timeline for milestones and goals.

*   **Data Management and Sharing Plan:**
    *   Must demonstrate responsiveness to the **FAIR** (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable) principles.
    *   Should include a discussion of the target audience for the data and a metric to assess data use/access.
    *   Should include a plan for comprehensive data management that ensures transparency, data sharing, and open-source software, including explicit open-source license(s) and repositories.
    *   It is helpful if data is “AI-ready” and machine learning models are made available with their training data.
    *   Digital data supporting published work is expected to be freely available without request within a reasonable time from publication.

*   **Single Copy Documents (Optional):**
    *   **Suggested Reviewers and Reviewers Not to Include:** List at least four suggested reviewers (with full names, affiliations, expertise, email) and/or up to four individuals to exclude. Suggested reviewers should not have conflicts of interest.

*   **Other Supplementary Documents:**
    *   **Letters of Collaboration:** For collaborations with researchers not identified as Senior/Key, federal, or international personnel, include letters confirming collaborations. These must follow PAPPG instructions (Chapter II, Section D.2.i) and may not contain statements of support or reference. Details of collaborative work should be in the Project Description. Letters are **not required** from DMREF’s federal partner agencies.
    *   **Cloud Computing Resources:** If requesting cloud computing resources through CloudBank, include a description (not to exceed 2 pages) with: (1) proposal title; (2) public cloud providers; (3) anticipated annual and total costs (with estimation method); and (4) technical description and justification for requested resources.

**Specific Instructions for International Partner Proposals:**

*   **Cover Sheet:**
    *   Check the “International Activity” box and select the corresponding collaborative country (Israel, India, Canada, or Germany).
    *   Personnel requesting funding from BSF, DST, NSERC, or DFG should **not** be listed on the cover sheet or designated as Senior/Key personnel on the NSF proposal.

*   **Project Summary and Description:**
    *   Must represent an integrated, joint effort.
    *   Include a substantive description of the collaboration, explaining the role(s) of the international collaborator(s) and how team members will work together.
    *   Describe intellectual merits, including the value of international collaboration, and anticipated societal benefits (Broader Impacts) relevant to the U.S. (and potentially the partner country).
    *   Describe the full proposed research program, including total U.S. and international resources.

*   **Budget:**
    *   U.S. PIs indicate only U.S. expenses on the NSF budget form.
    *   The BSF, DST, NSERC, or DFG budget and budget justification **must be included in the NSF proposal as a supplementary document**.
    *   The NSF Budget Justification section must clearly differentiate the U.S. budget from any similar funds requested by the international team and justify the full U.S. project budget. Duplicative funding requests may lead to return without review.

*   **Biographical Sketch:** Required for the equivalent of Senior/Key Personnel requesting funding from BSF, DST, NSERC, or DFG. Upload separately as a single PDF in "Other Supplementary Documents," clearly labeled.

*   **Collaborators and Other Affiliations (COA) Information:** Required for the equivalent of Senior/Key Personnel requesting funding from BSF, DST, NSERC, or DFG. Upload separately as a PDF in "Single Copy Documents" using the COA template, clearly labeled.

*   **Synergistic Activities & Current and Pending (Other) Support:** Not required for international collaborators (only for individuals requesting NSF funding).

*   **Results from Prior NSF Support:** Not required for international collaborators (only for individuals requesting NSF funding).

*   **Submission to Partner Agencies:**
    *   If the proposal contains a foreign investigator from Israel, India, or Germany, this individual **must** submit a copy of the NSF proposal to their respective agency (BSF, DST, or DFG) by their established deadline.
    *   Canadian investigators (NSERC) must submit a simplified application to the NSERC Alliance program by 8 PM (ET) on the final day of the NSF submission window.
    *   Failure to submit the international component to the foreign agency by the stated deadline will result in the international partner being treated as a non-funded collaborator.

## 8. Tips for Successful Proposals

*   **Emphasize the "Closed-Loop" Process:** Clearly articulate how theory, computational simulation, and experimental observation will iteratively guide each other. This is a core requirement and a key review criterion.
*   **Form an Interdisciplinary Team:** Ensure your team includes at least two Senior/Key Personnel with complementary expertise covering all necessary aspects: synthesis/growth/processing, characterization/testing, theory/mathematics, data science, and computation/simulation.
*   **Strong Data Management Plan:** Develop a comprehensive Data Management and Sharing Plan that adheres to the **FAIR principles** (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable). Detail how data will be made freely available, including open-source licenses and repositories. Consider making data "AI-ready" and sharing machine learning models.
*   **Workforce Development:** Describe substantial efforts to educate and train a diverse, next-generation workforce proficient in an integrated experimental and computational approach to materials research, consistent with MGI goals. Include plans for data-related training for students and postdocs.
*   **Align with MGI Strategic Plan:** Explicitly link your proposed research to the three primary goals of the 2021 MGI Strategic Plan: unifying the materials innovation infrastructure, harnessing materials data, and educating/training the workforce.
*   **Address National Priorities:** Demonstrate how your research aligns with Administration priorities for strengthening American leadership in critical technologies (e.g., microelectronics, AI, quantum information science, advanced manufacturing, biotechnology, clean energy, defense, homeland security, critical minerals, digital twins).
*   **Justify Collaborations:** If collaborating with federal or international partners, clearly explain the mutual benefits and how the collaboration advances project goals and MGI. Detail the implementation in the Management Plan.
*   **Realistic and Justified Budget:** Develop a budget that is consistent with the proposed activities and thoroughly justified. Remember to include travel funds for the biennial MGI PI meeting and any required federal partner meetings.
*   **Prior DMREF Awardees:** If you have a prior or existing DMREF award, clearly describe accomplishments in the "Results from Prior NSF Support" section, addressing Intellectual Merit, Broader Impacts, Data Management, and DMREF-specific criteria. Provide a clear explanation of MGI principles applied and a detailed vision for advancement.
