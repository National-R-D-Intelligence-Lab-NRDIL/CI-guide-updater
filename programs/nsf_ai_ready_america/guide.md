# NSF TechAccess: AI-Ready America

## 1. Executive Summary

TechAccess: AI-Ready America is a national-scale initiative by the U.S. National Science Foundation (NSF) to accelerate Artificial Intelligence (AI) readiness and adoption across the U.S. The program aims to strengthen coordination, leverage partnerships and resources, fill gaps, and scale effective approaches, enabling local and state priorities to shape an AI-driven economy that benefits all Americans.

This funding opportunity focuses on establishing **State/Territory Coordination Hubs**, which will form a coordinated national network of state and territorial hubs, partnerships, and pilot initiatives. These Hubs will expand AI literacy, workforce skills, and real-world AI adoption for communities, businesses, and governments.

The program anticipates making up to 56 awards, with each Coordination Hub receiving support of **$1M per year for three years**, with the possibility of an additional year.

## 2. Program Overview

TechAccess: AI-Ready America is designed to enable a future where every individual, community, and business thrives in an AI-driven economy by expanding access to AI knowledge, tools, training, and capacity building. Unlike initiatives solely focused on K-16 education, AI-Ready America additionally reaches businesses, public-serving organizations, and individuals, emphasizing practical implementation through hands-on assistance and workforce upskilling, including experiential learning.

The program is grounded in coordination, linking nationally developed resources and technical expertise with state-level leadership and local implementation to create a cohesive, adaptable AI infrastructure. It aligns with Administration priorities to promote AI literacy, integrate AI into education, and sustain America's global AI leadership.

**Vision:** To enable a future where every individual, community, and business thrives in an AI-driven economy.

**Strategic Approach:**
*   Strengthening coordination around AI readiness at local, state, and national levels in collaboration with community colleges and universities.
*   Leveraging existing networks and resources to accelerate AI adoption.
*   Identifying and addressing gaps that must be filled to advance AI readiness.

**Definition of "AI-Ready":** The continuum of literacy, proficiency, and fluency – the ability to understand, apply, and create with AI. "AI-literacy" starts with understanding "Why AI?" and "When AI?".

**Program Goals:**
*   Enable all Americans to be AI-literate and AI-ready.
*   Empower businesses — especially small and emerging enterprises — to adopt AI to increase competitiveness, productivity, and growth.
*   Support local governments and organizations in harnessing the power of AI to improve public services and spur local innovation.
*   Strengthen workforce capacity to apply and innovate with AI across education, business and economic sectors, and public services.

**Program Components:**
The AI-Ready America program consists of three distinct components:
1.  **State/Territory Coordination Hubs (Coordination Hubs)**: One in every state, the District of Columbia (DC), or territory in the United States. These Hubs connect partners, strengthen planning and deployment, and rapidly scale approaches. This funding opportunity focuses on Coordination Hubs.
2.  **National Coordination Lead (National Lead)**: Facilitates collaboration and knowledge sharing among Coordination Hubs, coordinates priority economic sectors, and informs national AI strategies. This will be funded as an Other Transaction (OT) offered through an Other Transaction Agreement Solutions Offering.
3.  **AI-Ready Catalyst Award Competitions**: A series of topic-driven competitions issued over the course of the program to pilot and scale innovative approaches that address critical national AI readiness needs. These will be announced through an NSF-approved mechanism.

**Coordination Hub Responsibilities (Primary Focus of this Solicitation):**
Coordination Hubs will serve as the center of state/territory-wide coordination to accelerate AI readiness and deployment. Their primary responsibilities include:
*   **AI Learning and Resource Navigator**: Maintain a publicly accessible inventory of state/territory AI-related resources.
*   **State/Territory AI Readiness Strategic Plan**: Develop a strategic plan in collaboration with partners, integrating data collection and evaluation.
*   **AI Deployment Support**: Provide hands-on assistance for AI adoption (advisory services, training, technical setup, integration, customization, deployment) to local governments, small businesses, and others. May develop an AI Deployment Corps.
*   **AI Readiness Training & Capacity Building**: Coordinate education, workforce, and industry partners to strengthen AI readiness, serving as a backbone organization to increase access to existing programs, scale successful efforts, and align pedagogy and credentialing. Expand access to experiential AI learning opportunities.
*   **Coordination Within Priority Sectors**: Identify and convene stakeholders in economic sectors critical to the state's economy (e.g., energy, agriculture, local manufacturing, healthcare delivery, education) to align AI adoption strategies, workforce training, and resource sharing.

**Federal Partners:**
The program is facilitated by the NSF Directorate for Technology, Innovation and Partnerships (TIP) in collaboration with the Directorates for STEM Education (EDU) and Computer and Information Science and Engineering (CISE). Additional federal partners include:
*   U.S. Department of Labor (DOL)
*   U.S. Department of Agriculture's National Institute of Food and Agriculture (USDA-NIFA)
*   U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA)

## 5. Award Size & Budget

**Anticipated Type of Award:**
*   Standard Grant or Continuing Grant.

**Estimated Number of Awards:**
*   Up to **56 awards**; one for every state, the District of Columbia, or territory in the U.S.
*   Coordination Hubs will be selected over three rounds: 10 in round one, 20 in round two, and the remainder in round three.

**Award Amount and Duration:**
*   Each Coordination Hub Award will receive support of **$1M per year for three years**.
*   There is a possibility for one additional year of support if the Coordination Hub can demonstrate a compelling need as it phases out or transitions to other sources of funding.

**Anticipated Funding Amount (Total Program):**
*   $168,000,000 to $224,000,000.
*   *Note: Estimated program budget, number of awards, and average award size/duration are subject to the availability of funds.*

**Budgetary Information:**
*   **Cost Sharing Requirements:** Inclusion of voluntary committed cost sharing is **prohibited**.
*   **Indirect Cost (F&A) Limitations:** Not Applicable.
*   **Other Budgetary Limitations:** Not Applicable.

## 6. How Proposals are Reviewed

All NSF proposals are evaluated through the two National Science Board-approved merit review criteria: Intellectual Merit and Broader Impacts. This solicitation also introduces additional review criteria tailored to the program's objectives.

**Merit Review Criteria:**
1.  **Intellectual Merit:** Encompasses the potential to advance knowledge.
2.  **Broader Impacts:** Encompasses the potential to benefit society and contribute to the achievement of specific, desired societal outcomes.

**Additional Solicitation Specific Review Criteria:**
Proposals will also be evaluated on the extent to which it is evident that the proposed State/Territory Coordination Hub:
*   Presents a clear vision and approach for advancing statewide AI readiness in alignment with program goals, national AI-readiness priorities, and responsibilities of the Coordination Hub.
*   Has a lead organization and partners that demonstrate statewide/territory-wide convening and coordination capacity, along with the expertise needed to implement the responsibilities effectively.
*   Reflects an understanding of current state/territory efforts in AI and related areas and offers strategies to address gaps and accelerate progress.
*   Includes realistic milestones, measurable outcomes, and mechanisms for evidence-based implementation and scaling of efforts.
*   Outlines credible strategies for mobilizing additional resources to expand impact beyond NSF funding.

**Review and Selection Process:**
Proposals will be reviewed by Ad hoc Review and/or Panel Review, Internal NSF Review, or Reverse Site Review. Partners listed under Participating Directorates and Agencies may participate in the proposal review process. After an initial compliance check, proposals will be reviewed by an NSF Program Officer, and in most cases, three or more external experts.

## 7. Application Requirements

All proposals must be submitted in accordance with the requirements specified in this funding opportunity and in the NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide (PAPPG) that is in effect for the relevant due date. The current version of the PAPPG (NSF 24-1) applies to all proposals submitted or due on or after May 20, 2024.

**A. Letters of Intent (Required)**
*   Must be submitted by 5:00 p.m. submitting organization's local time on the due date.
*   Letters of Intent will not be used as a pre-approval mechanism, and no feedback will be provided. They are used by NSF to assess requirements for proposal review.
*   Submission by an Authorized Organizational Representative (AOR) is not required.
*   Submission of multiple Letters of Intent is not permitted.
*   No Supplementary Documents are allowed for the Letter of Intent.

**Letter of Intent Content:**
*   **Title:** Must begin with "[Full name of state, DC, or territory] Coordination Hub", followed by the project title. The rest of the title should be precise, informative, and avoid acronyms.
*   **Project PI and Other Senior Project Personnel:** List full names and institutional affiliations for the PI, all Co-PIs, and Senior Personnel, including all sub-awardees. The PI's email address must be the point of contact for NSF inquiries.
*   **Participating Organizations:** List all institutions involved, including community partners.
*   **Synopsis:** Provide a one-page synopsis describing the work in sufficient detail to permit appropriate selection of potential reviewers.

**B. Full Proposal Preparation Instructions**
*   Proposers may opt to submit proposals via Research.gov (PAPPG guidelines apply) or Grants.gov (NSF Grants.gov Application Guide guidelines apply).
*   Collaborative proposals submitted as separate submissions from multiple organizations must be submitted via Research.gov.
*   Proposals must be prepared according to Chapter II.D.2 of the PAPPG, unless this solicitation specifies different instructions.

**Project Description Page Limit:**
*   Proposals are limited to **15 pages** for the Project Description. Proposals that exceed this limit will be returned without review. References do not count towards the page limit.

**Required Sections within the Project Description:**
Proposals must include Sections 1 – 5 using the specified section headers. Failure to comply with these five section headers will be grounds for Return Without Review (RWR).
*   **Section 1 - Vision and Approach to Responsibilities:** Describe your vision for advancing statewide AI readiness and how it aligns with program goals and the responsibilities noted in the Program Description.
*   **Section 2 - Organizational Background, Team Expertise, and Partnership Rationale:** Describe the lead organization's profile, team expertise, and partnership rationale.
*   **Section 3 - Current State of AI Planning and Coordination:** Provide an overview of existing efforts in the state/territory and explain how the proposed Coordination Hub will build on these efforts.
*   **Section 4 - Work Plan, Milestones, and Performance Metrics:** Present a clear work plan for each area of responsibility described in Section 1, including major milestones, timelines, and performance metrics.
*   **Section 5 - Resource Mobilization and Leveraging Additional Support:** Explain strategies for attracting additional resources to complement NSF funding and enable full implementation.

**Supplemental Materials:**
Proposals may include only those supplemental materials permitted by the NSF PAPPG and those explicitly required in this solicitation.
*   **Letters of Collaboration:** Required from all partners identified in the proposal, following the format described in the PAPPG.
*   **Data Management Plan:** Must be included, consistent with PAPPG requirements, as awards will involve data collection and reporting.
*   **Mentoring Plan:** Must be included if the proposal requests funding for postdoctoral researchers or graduate students.
*   No letters of support or additional narrative materials are permitted.

## 8. Tips for Successful Proposals

*   **Align with Program Goals:** Clearly articulate how your vision for statewide AI readiness aligns with the program's goals and the specific responsibilities of a Coordination Hub.
*   **Demonstrate Capacity and Expertise:** Ensure the lead organization demonstrates statewide convening power, operational capacity, and experience managing multi-sector initiatives. The team, including partners, should collectively bring the necessary AI expertise (technical understanding and practical implementation).
*   **Strategic Partnerships:** Assemble partnerships that best fit your state's priorities and leverage existing organizational structures and AI resources. Consider academic institutions (research universities, community colleges), workforce development organizations (American Job Centers), technology adoption entities (Cooperative Extension, Small Business Development Centers), and state/local government.
*   **Collaboration Structure:** Describe how the team will collaborate, including governance and decision-making structures. NSF encourages the use of at least one Advisory Board.
*   **Build on Existing Efforts:** Provide an overview of current AI readiness efforts in your state/territory and explain how your proposed Hub will build upon these, address gaps, and accelerate progress.
*   **Clear Work Plan and Metrics:** Present a clear work plan with major milestones, timelines, and measurable performance metrics for tracking progress. These metrics should include, but are not limited to:
    *   Number of individuals trained (by category).
    *   Number of small businesses and government entities assisted.
    *   Number of statewide convenings held.
    *   Contributions to national best-practice repositories.
    *   Number of individuals trained for and actively providing assistance through an AI Deployment Corps.
    *   Number of organizations provided technical assistance.
*   **Resource Mobilization:** Outline credible strategies for attracting additional financial or in-kind support from industry, philanthropy, state agencies, or other sources to expand impact and ensure eventual self-sustainment.
*   **Compliance with NSF Policies:** Ensure your proposal adheres to the requirements of the NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide (PAPPG) in effect for the due date, including page limits and required sections.
*   **Collaboration with National Lead:** As a condition of award, State/Territory Coordination Hubs will be required to actively collaborate with the National Coordination Lead once established. This includes participation in Hub convenings, contributing to national dashboards, engaging in AI Deployment Network activities, and coordinating sector-specific initiatives.
