# NSF GRANTED Sponsor Guide

## 1. Executive Summary

The U.S. National Science Foundation (NSF) Growing Research Access for Nationally Transformative Economic Development (GRANTED) program aims to strengthen and transform the administration of scientific research and workforce at U.S.-based organizations. It supports innovative models for research enterprise administrative development and workforce training infrastructure that promote sustainable research capacity and opportunities for economic impact. By providing access to resources in areas such as project idea development, federal funding priority updates, and grant submission and award management, GRANTED empowers researchers across the U.S. to compete in the global research enterprise.

## 2. Program Overview

*   **Program Name**: Growing Research Access for Nationally Transformative Economic Development (GRANTED)
*   **Purpose**: To strengthen and transform the administration of scientific research and workforce at U.S.-based organizations. The program supports innovative models of research enterprise administrative development and workforce training infrastructure that promote sustainable research capacity and opportunities for economic impact.
*   **Focus Areas**: GRANTED addresses the need for robust administrative support-and-service infrastructure within the research enterprise, which broadly includes:
    *   Research development and administration
    *   Research analytics
    *   Technology transfer and commercialization
    *   Corporate relations/public-private partnerships
    *   Research integrity, compliance and security
    *   Research policy
    *   Administration of student research training
    *   Research leadership
*   **Key Themes**: Proposals must be centered around one or more of the following three main themes:
    1.  Innovating and enhancing practices and processes within the research enterprise.
    2.  Developing and strengthening human capital within the research enterprise.
    3.  Translating effective practices related to the research enterprise into a broad range of organizational contexts.
*   **Exclusions**: GRANTED is not intended to fund discipline-specific STEM research and training projects.
*   **Expected Outcomes**: Collectively, proposals funded through this Program Description (NSF 23-221Y) are expected to advance the transformation of the national research enterprise, measured through:
    1.  Generating scalable models that improve research capacity and competitiveness.
    2.  Creating collaborations, partnerships, and communities centered around strengthening the Nation's research enterprise.
    3.  Increasing the range of project leadership, organizations, ideas, and approaches that NSF funds, especially related to developing areas aligned to agency priorities.
    4.  Strengthening engagement across the Nation’s research enterprise.
*   **Administering Office**: Office of Integrative Activities (OD/OIA)

## 4. Eligibility

The GRANTED program utilizes general proposal requirements, including eligibility, as outlined in the current NSF Proposal and Award Policies and Procedures Guide (PAPPG). The funding opportunity is open to a broad array of projects relevant to the research enterprise.

## 5. Award Size & Budget

The project budget and duration should be determined by the scope of the proposed activities and presented in accordance with the NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide (PAPPG). A justification, typically up to 5 pages long, must document and justify each budget line item included in the proposal budget.

## 6. How Proposals are Reviewed

The provided sources do not explicitly detail the review criteria or scoring for the GRANTED program. However, general NSF proposals typically include statements on **intellectual merit** and **broader impacts** in the project summary. Proposals should aim to achieve the program's expected outcomes, which include generating scalable models, creating collaborations, increasing the range of project leadership/organizations/ideas, and strengthening engagement across the national research enterprise.

## 7. Application Requirements

All proposals must be submitted in accordance with the requirements specified in the GRANTED Program Description (NSF 23-221Y) and the NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide (PAPPG) that is in effect for the relevant due date. It is the responsibility of the proposer to ensure the proposal meets these requirements.

*   **General Guidance**:
    *   Follow Chapter II of the PAPPG (II.D for research proposals, II.F for other types of proposals).
    *   If applying in response to a specific program solicitation or broad agency announcement, follow the guidelines outlined in that document if they modify standard PAPPG guidelines.
*   **Submission Platforms**:
    *   Full proposals submitted via Research.gov: NSF PAPPG proposal preparation guidelines apply.
    *   Full proposals submitted via Grants.gov: NSF Grants.gov Application Guide guidelines apply.
*   **Common Parts of an NSF Proposal** (Refer to PAPPG Chapter II for full requirements):
    *   **Project Summary**: A 1-page overview of the proposed activity, including statements on its intellectual merit and broader impacts (PAPPG II.D.2.b).
    *   **Project Description**: Typically up to 15 pages, detailing what the proposer wants to do, why, how, how success will be measured, and potential benefits (PAPPG II.D.2.d).
    *   **References Cited**: Includes bibliographic citations only; cannot be used for parenthetical information outside the project description (PAPPG II.D.2.e).
    *   **Documents Required for Senior/Key Personnel** (PAPPG II.2.D.h):
        *   **Biographical Sketch**: Outlines education, training, appointments, and qualifications.
        *   **Current and Pending (Other) Support**: Lists proposed and active projects and sources of support to assess capacity, potential scientific and budgetary overlap or duplication, and overcommitment.
        *   **Collaborators and Other Affiliations**: A table of an individual's collaborators, such as advisors, co-authors, and other collaborators.
        *   **Synergistic Activities**: A one-page document with up to five examples demonstrating the broader impact of an individual's professional and scholarly activities.
    *   **Proposal Budget and Justification**: Details requested funds by category across each year of support. A justification, typically up to 5 pages, must document and justify each budget line item (PAPPG II.D.2.f).
    *   **Facilities, Equipment and Other Resources**: Outlines internal and external resources (physical and personnel) provided by the proposing organization and its collaborators (PAPPG II.D.2.g).
    *   **Mentoring Plan** (if applicable): A 1-page summary describing mentoring activities for graduate students or postdoctoral researchers, if funding is requested for their support (PAPPG II.D.2.i(i)).
    *   **Data Management and Sharing Plan**: Up to two pages, describing how the proposal will follow NSF policy on disseminating and sharing research results. Solicitations may outline specific requirements (PAPPG II.D.2.i(ii)).
*   **Research Security Policies**: Proposers must be aware of updates issued on July 10, 2025, which include a research security training requirement, Malign Foreign Talent Recruitment Program annual certification requirement, prohibition on Confucius institutes, and an updated FFDR reporting and submission timeline.

## 8. Tips for Successful Proposals

*   **Adhere to Guidelines**: Always follow the guidelines in the NSF Proposal and Award Policies and Procedures Guide (PAPPG) and any specific program solicitations or broad agency announcements.
*   **Contact Program Personnel**: Prospective PIs are strongly encouraged to contact GRANTED initiative personnel (granted@nsf.gov) with inquiries prior to developing and submitting a proposal.
*   **Engage Administrative Workforce**: Proposals should engage the professional, administrative research support-and-service workforce in project leadership roles described within proposals.
*   **Focus on Broad Impact**: Proposed projects should look beyond individual and discipline-specific research needs and focus on activities that create organization-wide impact and potential for regional and national impact.
*   **Propose Scalable Solutions**: Projects should propose scalable approaches and models to build and sustain research enterprise infrastructure.
*   **Address Challenges**: Competitive proposals will recognize structural and organizational challenges and include goals to implement interventions, solutions, and/or strategies that will mitigate these challenges.
*   **Utilize Office Hours**: Participate in NSF GRANTED office hour sessions to receive guidance and support, including advice, answers to questions, and clarification on application requirements and procedures.
