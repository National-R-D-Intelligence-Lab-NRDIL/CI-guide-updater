# NSF CAREER Award Sponsor Guide

## 1. Executive Summary

The Faculty Early Career Development (CAREER) Program is the National Science Foundation's (NSF) most prestigious award in support of early-career faculty. It is a Foundation-wide activity designed to support faculty who have the potential to serve as academic role models in research and education and to lead advances in the mission of their department or organization. The program aims to help early-career faculty build a firm foundation for a lifetime of leadership in integrating education and research.

## 2. Program Overview

The CAREER program supports early-career faculty in developing a lifetime of leadership in integrating education and research. NSF encourages proposals from early-career faculty at all CAREER-eligible organizations, with particular encouragement for women, members of underrepresented minority groups, and persons with disabilities to apply.

The program also includes the description of the NSF component of the Presidential Early Career Awards for Scientists and Engineers (PECASE). Each year, NSF selects nominees for PECASE from among the most meritorious recent CAREER awardees. Individuals cannot apply for PECASE; these awards are initiated by participating federal agencies. At NSF, up to twenty-six nominees are selected annually from PECASE-eligible CAREER awardees.

## 4. Eligibility

*   **Principal Investigator (PI)**: Proposals may only be submitted by early-career faculty who meet specific PI eligibility requirements detailed in the solicitation.
*   **Proposal Limit**: An eligible Principal Investigator may submit only one CAREER proposal per annual competition.
*   **Co-PIs**: No co-Principal Investigators are permitted.
*   **Encouraged Applicants**: NSF especially encourages women, members of underrepresented minority groups, and persons with disabilities to apply.

## 5. Award Size & Budget

*   **Estimated Number of Awards**: Approximately 500 awards are made per year.
*   **Award Amount**: The annual amount is approximate, includes new and continuing increments, and is subject to the availability of funds. (Specific dollar amounts for individual awards are not provided in the source material.)

## 6. How Proposals are Reviewed

The provided sources do not explicitly detail the review criteria or scoring for the initial CAREER proposal submissions.

However, the selection for the **Presidential Early Career Awards for Scientists and Engineers (PECASE)**, for which CAREER awardees are eligible, is based on two criteria:
1.  Performance of innovative research at the frontiers of science, engineering, and technology that is relevant to the mission of the sponsoring organization or agency.
2.  Community service demonstrated through scientific leadership, education, or community outreach.

## 7. Application Requirements

*   **General Submission**: All proposals must be submitted in accordance with the requirements specified in the funding opportunity (NSF 22-586) and the NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide (PAPPG) that is in effect for the relevant due date. Proposers are responsible for ensuring their proposal meets these requirements.
*   **PAPPG**: The current version, NSF 24-1, applies to all proposals submitted or due on or after May 20, 2024. Supplements (NSF 26-200 and NSF 26-202) implement further changes for financial assistance awarded on or after December 8, 2025, and January 22, 2026, respectively.
*   **Research Security Policies**: As of July 10, 2025, NSF has updated its research security policies, including:
    *   A research security training requirement.
    *   A Malign Foreign Talent Recruitment Program annual certification requirement.
    *   A prohibition on Confucius institutes.
    *   An updated FFDR reporting and submission timeline.
*   **Key Personnel**: No co-PIs are permitted on CAREER proposals.
*   **Proposal Limit**: A Principal Investigator may submit only one CAREER proposal per annual competition.

## 8. Tips for Successful Proposals

*   **Integrate Research and Education**: Activities pursued by early-career faculty should aim to build a firm foundation for a lifetime of leadership in integrating education and research.
*   **Diversity and Inclusion**: NSF encourages proposals from early-career faculty at all CAREER-eligible organizations and especially encourages women, members of underrepresented minority groups, and persons with disabilities to apply.
*   **Adhere to Guidelines**: Ensure your proposal strictly adheres to the requirements specified in the latest solicitation (NSF 22-586) and the NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide (PAPPG).
