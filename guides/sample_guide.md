# Sponsor Guide — NIH Research Enhancement Award (R15)

## Program Overview

The NIH Research Enhancement Award (R15) supports small-scale, meritorious
research projects at educational institutions that have not been major
recipients of NIH funding. The program aims to strengthen the research
environment and expose students to biomedical and behavioral research.

There are two distinct R15 programs:

- **AREA** (Academic Research Enhancement Award) — for undergraduate-focused
  institutions.
- **REAP** (Research Enhancement Award Program) — for health professional
  schools and graduate schools.

## Eligibility

- **Applicant institutions** must be domestic (U.S.) organizations.
- For AREA: the academic component must have greater undergraduate than
  graduate student enrollment.
- For REAP: combined organizations may not have received more than $6 million
  per year in NIH support (total costs).
- A signed letter from the Provost (or equivalent) is required for REAP
  applications.

## Budget & Duration

- Up to **$300,000** in direct costs over the entire project period.
- Project periods of up to **3 years**.

## Submission Requirements

- Applications must be submitted via ASSIST, Grants.gov, or an
  institutional system-to-system solution.
- PD/PI must hold a full-time appointment at the applicant institution.
- Only **one R15 application per PD/PI** may be pending or active at a time.

## Review Criteria

Applications are evaluated on the following standard NIH review criteria:

1. Significance
2. Investigator(s)
3. Innovation
4. Approach
5. Environment
